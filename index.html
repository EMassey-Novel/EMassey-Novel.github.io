The Forest is Woven

A novel about perception, control, and resistance, and what it costs to keep trusting your own perspective.

In a near-future city governed by the architecture of augmentation, wellness systems, and predictive control, Elias begins noticing patterns the system was designed to ignore.

When he is hired to analyze the writings of a young woman whose unique connection to the information aether was classified as malfunction, Elias becomes part of a quiet, distributed network of people mapping the ways around a technological society optimized for compliance.

What begins with a forgotten post on an archived forum becomes a quiet act of preservation, carried through fragments, buried channels, and minds the architecture couldn't force to comply.

About the Author

Edwin Massey is a computer scientist working in AI Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback. Living with autism and chronic pain, and spending his days in close interaction with AI systems, he writes from a particular angle on perception, consciousness, and what it costs to see clearly in a world that often disregards what does not fit.

The Forest is Woven is his first novel.
