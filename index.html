Edwin Massey

The Forest is Woven

A literary speculative novel about perception, control, and the hidden networks that survive beneath systems built to smooth everything flat.

In a near-future city governed by architecture, wellness systems, and predictive control, Elias begins noticing patterns the system was designed to ignore.

When he discovers Mara's journal, written by a young woman whose sensitivity was classified as malfunction, he becomes part of a quiet, distributed atlas of people mapping the blind spots of a world optimized for compliance.

What begins as systems analysis becomes a record of resistance, carried through fragments, through buried channels, and through minds the architecture never learned how to classify.
